## Preface

This book exists because teachers have tried Claude and found it useful in ways they did not fully trust. A lesson plan appears in thirty seconds. A feedback draft sounds plausible. A differentiated activity materializes on request. The outputs look like the product of professional judgment. The question this book is designed to answer is: what is actually yours, and what still has to be?

*Claude for Education: A Practitioner's Guide* is written for educators who want a disciplined method, not a tool review. The central tension is real: Claude can make educational artifacts easier to produce, but artifacts are not learning. Speed is not quality. Fluency is not understanding. The teacher's job is to define what learning is for, protect the cognitive struggle that learning requires, and decide whether any given AI output is worth acting on.

This book moves through three acts. Act One grounds Claude use in learning outcomes and teacher judgment — the things the machine cannot supply. Act Two builds practical workflows for planning, feedback, differentiation, rubrics, and materials, each with teacher review gates. Act Three addresses student use, assessment redesign, privacy, equity, and policy — the classroom system that makes principled AI use sustainable.

The book was written as part of the Humanitarians AI educational program, under the direction of Nik Bear Brown, Northeastern University Associate Teaching Professor and founder of Humanitarians AI Incorporated.
