## FERPA/Privacy Check (Ch 09 and Ch 11) — Special Note

The FERPA-related claims across Chapters 9 and 11 **check out**. Specifically:

- The statutory citation (20 U.S.C. § 1232g; 34 CFR Part 99) is correct.
- The description of covered record types (grades, transcripts, enrollment info, financial aid, disciplinary records) is accurate under FERPA's definition of "education records."
- The core guidance — that pasting identifiable student records into a third-party AI system without documented institutional approval is risky and requires checking — is accurate and appropriately hedged.
- The chapter does not claim FERPA categorically prohibits all Claude use with student data; it correctly says approval "may exist" via enterprise agreements and directs readers to verify. This is responsible framing.
- The observation that entering student records into Claude "may constitute a disclosure under FERPA" is appropriately hedged — FERPA's disclosure provisions have exceptions and depend on institutional agreements.
- The international equivalents (GDPR, Canadian provincial law, Australian law) are mentioned as parallel frameworks without overstating their specific rules.
- The chapter's practical advice (anonymize, aggregate, describe patterns rather than individuals) is consistent with standard FERPA compliance practice guidance from the U.S. Department of Education's Student Privacy Policy Office (studentprivacy.ed.gov).

No FERPA-related claims require correction. The optional precision note above (Ch 09: "belong to the student" vs. "FERPA confers access rights") is a refinement, not an error.
